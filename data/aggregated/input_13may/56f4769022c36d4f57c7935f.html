Poll: Here's how the Republican convention could get ugly this summer

Josh Hafner, USA TODAY 4:03 p.m. EDT March 23, 2016
69 Shares

Trump speaks at a campaign rally in New Orleans on March 4, 2016. (Photo: Gerald Herbert, AP)

A new poll released Wednesday from Monmouth University underscores the possible schism that could unfold at this summer’s Republican convention.

Fifty-four percent of Republicans say their party should get behind front-runner Donald Trump at a contested convention if he still has the most delegates come July 18.

Thirty-four percent of Republican voters would want to see another candidate nominated.

Among Republicans who would want a candidate besides Trump nominated at a contested convention, 33% think Ted Cruz should get the nod, while 23% back John Kasich.

(Also-rans and non-candidates like Marco Rubio, Mitt Romney and Paul Ryan got some support, too.)

“A majority of non-Trump supporters seem to be in favor of a brokered convention process at this point in the campaign,” said Patrick Murray, director of Monmouth’s Polling Institute. “

“That would probably throw the party into turmoil with many Trump supporters abandoning the party.”

Meanwhile, another new poll out Wednesday showed Kasich standing a better chance in the general election than Trump, and Democratic underdog Bernie Sanders faring better in November than his rival, Hillary Clinton.
