By: Andrew Joseph | April 26, 2016 8:38 pm Follow @andyj0seph

It’s often a risky game when politicians attempt to discuss sports because it opens themselves up to completely irrelevant scrutiny.

But Ted Cruz made it too easy.

During a rally at the Hoosier Gym Community Center in Knightstown, Ind., Cruz referred to a basketball hoop as a “basketball ring” right there in that famous gym.

@DavidMacAnally Cruz reenact a famous HOOSIERS scene to reassure Indiana supporters. Remember this? pic.twitter.com/Hz7nW2lV3I

— David MacAnally (@DavidMacAnally) April 26, 2016

He was measuring the hoop in an attempt to recreate this scene from Hoosiers.

It wasn’t referred to as a “ring” in the film, though.

Cruz could have called the hoop a goal, rim, basket or a cylinder (weird, but correct), and nobody would have blinked an eye. But a ring?

That isn’t right. That isn’t right at all.

No one has ever called it a "basketball ring" before Cruz just now in the history of basketball, politics, or words.

— luke oneil (@lukeoneil47) April 26, 2016
