South Carolina, Nevada Vote on Presidential Candidates

Hillary Clinton edges out Bernie Sanders in Nevada, while Donald Trump wins South Carolina; Republican candidate Jeb Bush suspends campaign

Updated Feb. 21, 2016 12:59 a.m. ET 0 COMMENTS

fullscreen
Businessman and Republican presidential candidate Donald Trump—flanked by his daughter Ivanka, far left, and wife Melania, far right—waits to speak to his supporters in Spartanburg, S.C., after his victory in the South Carolina Republican primary. Jonathan Enst/Reuters …
Democratic presidential candidate Hillary Clinton, left, greets supporters with her husband and former President Bill Clinton at a Nevada Democratic caucus rally on Saturday. JOHN LOCHER/ASSOCIATED PRESS …
Florida Sen. Marco Rubio, accompanied by his family, waves to his supporters in at a rally in Columbia, S.C., after the Republican primary. Mr. Rubio posted a second-place finish in the primary. Chris Keane/Reuters …
Following the results of the South Carolina Republican primary, former Florida Gov. Jeb Bush, accompanied by his wife, Columba, said he was suspending his campaign for the presidential nomination at a rally in Columbia, S.C. Matt Rourke/Associated Press …
South Carolina Governor Nikki Haley, left, greets Mr. Rubio before his speech in Columbia, S.C., following the South Carolina Republican primary. Win McNamee/Getty Images …
After the South Carolina Republican primary, presidential candidate Sen. Ted Cruz (R., Texas) addresses supporters at a rally in Columbia, S.C. Joshua Roberts/Reuters …
Mr. Sanders speaks to his supporters in Henderson, Nev., after Mrs. Clinton was projected as the winner. ‘Taking on the establishment, whether it is the financial establishment, whether it is the political establishment, whether it is the media establishment, is not easy,’ he told his supporters. Jim Young/Reuters …
Republican presidential candidate and Ohio Governor John Kasich addresses the crowd in Wakefield, Mass., at a ‘watch party’ for results from the South Carolina Republican primary. Brian Snyder/Reuters …
In Spartanburg, S.C., Mr. Trump’s supporters celebrate the positive early results of the South Carolina Republican primary. Richard Ellis/European Pressphoto Agency …
Mrs. Clinton’s supporters in Las Vegas cheer before she was projected by the media as the caucus winner. Josh Edelson/Agence France-Presse/Getty Images …
Caucus-goers register at the Del Sol Academy of the Performing Arts in Las Vegas. Mrs. Clinton’s lead in Nevada shrank as some women voters switched to Mr. Sanders. John Gurzinski/Agence France-Presse/Getty Images …
Saturday gave voters in the Western and Southern U.S. their first chance to pass judgment on this year’s presidential candidates, with the South Carolina Republican primary and the Nevada Democratic caucuses. The previous votes were held in New Hampshire and Iowa. Above, a precinct official counts votes of caucus-goers at Rancho High School in Las Vegas. Mike Nelson/European Pressphoto Agency …
GOP officials predicted a high turnout in South Carolina, and absentee voting backed up that expectation. Almost 59,000 absentee ballots were turned in by Friday, according to a spokesman for the state’s election commission. That was more than the 2008 and 2012 results combined. Above, a voter casts his ballot at Our Savior Lutheran Church in Columbia, S.C. Daniel Acker/Bloomberg News …
Republican voters wait to cast ballots at an American Legion Hall in Cayce, S.C. Erik S. Lesser/European Pressphoto Agency …
Mr. Sanders and his wife Jane, left, greet voters at a caucus site in Las Vegas. Jim Young/Reuters …
Ms. Haley is shown voting in Lexington, S.C. The remaining Republican presidential candidates were looking to make up ground on Mr. Trump the party’s front-runner. Ms. Haley had previously endorsed Mr. Rubio. Matt Rourke/Associated Press …
Mr. Bush, left, gets a hug from his mother, former first lady Barbara Bush, after visiting a polling station in Greenville, S.C. Rainier Ehrhardt/Reuters …
Mr. Trump was the top pick of 28% of likely Republican primary voters in South Carolina, according to a Wall Street Journal/NBC News/Marist poll held in the final days before Saturday’s election, but that was down 8 percentage points from a January survey. Above, voters cast ballots at the fire department in Mauldin, S.C. Paul Sancya/Associated Press …
People were out in support of their preferred Democratic candidate outside a caucus location at Caesars Palace in Las Vegas. Nevada has 23 delegates up for grabs in this year’s caucuses. David Becker/Reuters …
South Carolina awards 29 delegates to the statewide winner of the GOP primary and another three to the winner of each of its seven congressional districts. Above, a voter casts her ballot at Hand Middle School in Columbia. Alex Wong/Getty Images …
Ballots were cast in the Horse Gall voting station, which was in the garage of precinct volunteer David Smith in Varnville, S.C. There are 76 registered voters in the Horse Gall precinct. Randall Hill/Reuters …
