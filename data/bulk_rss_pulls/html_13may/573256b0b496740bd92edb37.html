  • BROOKFIELD - The sun finally came out yesterday, with unfortunate consequences for the owners of an antique farmhouse overlooking the town common.
    A magnifying shaving mirror left on an outside deck reflected a beam of sunlight into the side of the wooden house, causing a three-alarm blaze, said Brookfield’s fire chief, Peter E. Martell.
    No one was injured in the fire that caused extensive damage to the house at 17 River St., but homeowners Sean and Margo Mulligan and a tenant had to be relocated to temporary housing by the Red Cross, Chief Martell said.
    He said Mr. Mulligan was at home at the time of the fire, which was reported around 2:30 p.m. Monday - a day when balmy weather had returned after a long stretch of gray skies and rain.
    “The first day in 10 days when the sun was a factor - unfortunately for him,” said the chief.
    Chief Martell said colleagues from Spencer and Sturbridge and the state fire marshal’s office joined in the investigation into the cause of the blaze.
    The culprit was found to be the shaving mirror that had been left outside on a bench on the deck off the kitchen.
    “The mirror reflected the sunlight onto the wood and, slowly but surely, started the fire,” he said.
    He said smoke detectors were in place and working in the home at the time of the fire, which was reported by a passerby who saw smoke coming from the building.
